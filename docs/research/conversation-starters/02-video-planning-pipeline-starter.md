### Key Strategic Insights (March 2026)

These insights were synthesised from 13 creator/strategy videos and directly shape the pipeline design:

**From Rory Sutherland:** "Don't sell what you do. Sell how you think." Content about *how to think about AI problems* will always outperform content about *how to do AI things*. The 180-degree flip — taking a commonly held assumption and arguing the opposite — is an endlessly repeatable browse format. Every video should pass the test: does this showcase a perspective, or just information?

**From One-of-One Creator:** The replaceability test should be a quality gate at Stage 1: "Could 100 other creators have made this video?" If yes, find the angle that makes it uniquely Rich's. Hyperpersonal storytelling — sharing real experiences, specific war stories from that week — is what creates irreplaceable content.

**From Raw/Authentic research (multiple videos):** The idea is 80% of whether a video succeeds (title + thumbnail + idea). Production quality is table stakes, not a differentiator. The algorithm in 2026 actively rewards authentic, conversational content — especially for older audiences (higher CPM). Walk-and-talk format is a legitimate, proven format.

**From Speaking Skills research:** The coffee shop rule (would you say this to a friend in a café?). Paint every point with analogy, metaphor, story, or example. Set one emotional target before recording. Warm up body and mind before hitting record. Use bullet points, not scripts — film the same day you outline.

**From Daniel Priestley:** Pain/prize/news as a repeatable hook framework. The "Pause Reflect Document" exercise (scroll 60 months of photos, extract stories with lessons) as an idea generation method.

**From Tom Gregory (personal call):** Aim for browse algorithm. Create content appealing to a wider developer/tech enthusiast audience. The story is the asset, not just the technical knowledge. Browse = emotional hook + curiosity + wide relatability. Search = answer a specific query someone is typing.

## The Pipeline Stages

### Stage 1: Idea Capture & Classification
**Input:** Raw idea — could be a voice memo from a forest walk, a problem hit during work, something from X/HackerNews, a story from the week.

**AI assistance needed:**
- Classify: Is this a browse or search idea? (Or both?)
- Which content pillar does it belong to?
- Initial audience assessment — who cares about this and why?
- Quick check: Has this been covered to death already? Is there a fresh angle?
- **NEW: Replaceability test** — Could 100 other creators make this video? If yes, what makes Rich's version unique?
- **NEW: "How you think" test** — Does this showcase a perspective/framework, or just deliver information? Perspective content compounds; information content commoditises.
- **NEW: One-of-one check** — Does this draw on Rich's specific lived experience that nobody else has?

**Output:** Idea card with classification, initial angle, "worth pursuing" score (1-10), and one-of-one rating.

### Stage 2: Validation & Research
**Input:** Idea card from Stage 1.

**AI assistance needed:**
- **For search content:** What are people actually searching for around this topic? Keyword variants? Search volume indicators?
- **For browse content:** What's the emotional trigger? What makes someone click this on their homepage? What's the curiosity gap?
- **NEW: Emotional target** — Name the primary emotion: relief, curiosity, aspiration, hope, recognition ("that's me!"). This guides everything downstream.
- **Competitive scan:** Who else has covered this? What did they do well/poorly? Where's my unique angle?
- **NEW: Current news hook** — Is there a recent article, study, or event that validates this topic? (e.g., IEEE Spectrum's AI coding failures article, Fortune's AI agent reliability piece). News hooks dramatically increase browse algorithm relevance.
- **Timeliness:** Is this time-sensitive? Is there a news hook?
- **Feasibility:** Do I have the footage/knowledge/access to make this well?

**Output:** Validated idea brief with research notes, competitive context, emotional target, news hooks, and go/no-go recommendation.

### Stage 3: Hook & Title Development
**Input:** Validated idea brief.

**AI assistance needed:**
- Generate 5-10 title candidates (mix of browse-optimised and search-optimised)
- Evaluate each title against known patterns:
  - **Browse titles:** Curiosity gap, emotional trigger, specificity, number/age anchors ("I'm 52 and...", "After 25 Years...")
  - **Search titles:** Keyword-first, clear value proposition, year tag
  - **NEW: 180-degree flip titles** (Sutherland format) — counterintuitive claim that makes the viewer need to hear the argument
- Craft the hook — the first 15-30 seconds that determine whether viewers stay
- Hook archetypes to explore: Problem/solution, story opening, bold claim, question, pattern interrupt
- **NEW: Pain/prize/news hook framework** (Priestley) — which of the three types is this hook?
- Thumbnail concept notes (what visual would pair with each title)
  - **NEW: Keep thumbnails to 3-5 words max. Screenshots often outperform designed thumbnails for walk-and-talk format.**

**Output:** Top 3 title/hook combinations with thumbnail direction. Recommended hook should be practised aloud (coffee shop rule test).

### Stage 4: Three-Act Structure
**Input:** Selected title/hook from Stage 3.

**AI assistance needed:**
- Structure the video using a 3-act framework:
  - **Act 1 (Setup):** Hook → context → promise of what viewer will learn/experience
  - **Act 2 (Confrontation):** The meat — problem exploration, demonstration, story development, key points
  - **Act 3 (Resolution):** Payoff, key takeaway, call to action, bridge to next video
- Estimate duration for each act (target total: 8-15 minutes for browse, 5-12 for tutorials)
- Identify "retention checkpoints" — moments designed to re-engage viewers who might click away
- Plan B-roll / screen recording / demo needs per section
- Identify the "golden moment" — the single most compelling/shareable moment in the video
- **NEW: Identify "ad-lib zones"** — sections where Rich should speak from conviction rather than following bullets
- **NEW: Mark "paint" opportunities** — where should an analogy, metaphor, story, or example be used to make a point land?

**Output:** Structured outline with timing, visual notes, retention strategy, and golden moment identified.

### Stage 5: Bullet-Point Script
**Input:** Three-act structure from Stage 4.

**AI assistance needed:**
- Generate a bullet-point script (NOT a word-for-word script — I need to sound natural)
- Each bullet = a talking point with:
  - Key phrase or sentence to hit
  - Supporting detail or example
  - Visual/demo note (what should be on screen)
  - Transition to next point
- Opening and closing should be more scripted (these matter most)
- Include "ad-lib zones" where I should just talk naturally about my experience
- Flag any claims that need verification or demonstrations that need preparation
- **NEW: Include [KEY BEAT] markers** for the most important moments that must not be missed
- **NEW: Include [PAINT] markers** where an analogy, metaphor, or story should illustrate the point

**Output:** Filmable bullet-point script with production notes.

### Stage 6: Production Notes (NEW)
**Input:** Completed bullet-point script.

**Output:** Practical filming checklist:
- **Re-record vs edit existing?** Decision framework based on whether raw footage has clean structure
- **Format:** Walk-and-talk / screen recording / hybrid / static setup
- **Audio:** Clip-on mic status (the one non-negotiable technical minimum)
- **Gear:** DJI Osmo Pocket + extension rod for walk-and-talk; MacBook screen recording for demos
- **Thumbnail:** Screenshot from recording + 3-5 word text overlay (Canva is fine)
- **Editing approach:** Minimal — trim dead air/false starts. No music, no B-roll, no fancy edits unless specifically needed
- **Warm-up ritual:** Body shake-out (2 min) + mind warm-up (visualise the benefit of making this video)
- **Coffee shop check:** Say the opening aloud — would it sound natural in a café conversation?

## Pipeline Variants

### Browse Video Pipeline
Emphasis on: emotional hook, story arc, relatability, curiosity gap, one-of-one positioning
Title pattern: "I [did X] and [surprising result]" / "Why [bold claim] After [timeframe]" / "[Age/experience] [action] [unexpected topic]"
Structure: Story-driven, personal narrative woven through educational content
Script style: More ad-lib zones, personal anecdotes, emotional beats
**NEW — "How you think" test:** Every browse video should leave the viewer with a *framework for thinking about something*, not just information about it.

### Search Video Pipeline
Emphasis on: keyword targeting, clear value proposition, comprehensive coverage
Title pattern: "[Topic]: [Specific benefit/answer]" / "How to [action] with [tool] in [year]" / "[Tool A] vs [Tool B]: [Decisive comparison]"
Structure: Problem → solution → demonstration → tips → summary
Script style: More structured, step-by-step, screen recordings dominant

### Hybrid Video Pipeline
Browse title + search content. Example: "I Built a Robot Tutor for My Daughter — Here's How" (browse hook, but contains searchable tutorial content)
**This is Rich's strongest format** — the one-of-one story as the container, with genuinely useful technical content inside.

## Proven Pipeline Output: Twin AI Paradoxes (March 2026)

The pipeline was successfully run for the first time on 2026-03-26, producing a complete video plan for "The Twin AI Paradoxes" — a browse video based on Rich's walk-and-talk forest recording. The full plan is at `/video-plans/001-twin-ai-paradoxes.md`.

**Key learnings from the proof of concept:**
1. The pipeline works well as a structured conversation in Claude Desktop — interactive, not templated
2. Raw walk-and-talk transcripts contain excellent raw material but need structural reorganisation
3. The recommendation was to re-record (not edit existing footage) because the original lacked structure — but the stories were gold
4. Current news hooks (found via web search during Stage 2) significantly strengthened the validation
5. The plan doubles as a practical teleprompter — bullet points on phone during the walk
6. Total time to produce the plan: ~30 minutes of Claude interaction
7. The "Why This Video First" section at the end proved valuable for decision-making confidence

**What makes a good first video (validated criteria):**
- It's one-of-one content — nobody else has these stories
- It sells how you think (Sutherland) — the framing IS a way of thinking
- It's emotionally resonant — relief for anxious engineers
- It's browse-algorithm material — wide appeal, emotional hook, curiosity gap
- It establishes positioning — practitioner, not pundit
- It's low friction to produce — walk-and-talk format
- It signals what the channel is about — setting the algorithm up from video one

## Existing Patterns to Leverage

### From GuardKit Work
The Research-to-Implementation Handoff Template established a pattern of:
- Front-loading decisions in Phase 1 (research)
- Structuring output for Phase 2 consumption (implementation)
- Decision logs, task breakdowns, quality gates

The video planning pipeline should follow the same philosophy:
- Front-load creative decisions (hook, structure, key moments)
- Structure the output so filming is pure execution (not creative decision-making on the fly)
- Quality gates at each stage (is the hook strong enough? Is the structure sound?)

### From the Conversation Starter Pattern
- Each stage should be a discrete, completable unit
- Clear handoff between stages
- Human-in-the-loop at key decision points (go/no-go, title selection)
- AI handles the heavy lifting (research, generation, evaluation)

- **Hook patterns:** Story opening, bold claim, pattern interrupt, pain/prize/news
- **Title patterns:** Curiosity gap, 180-degree flip, age anchor, first-person authority
- **Thumbnail patterns:** 3-5 words max, screenshots often beat designed, split visual for contrast
- **Script patterns:** Bullet points not scripts, film same day as outline, coffee shop rule
- **Production patterns:** Walk-and-talk format, clip-on mic as minimum, no fancy editing needed
- **Quality tests:** Replaceability test, "how you think" test, coffee shop rule, emotional target
